##🌟 Revivo — Brand Story

Revivo began with a simple idea:
when we remember more, we connect better — and when we connect better, life feels fuller.

In a world where moments slip through the cracks and relationships fade under the weight of busy days, Revivo gently gathers the threads: your conversations, places, diary entries, voices, people, and milestones. It helps you look back with clarity, reach out with intention, and revive the connections that give your life its shape.

Revivo is not just an AI assistant.
It’s a quiet presence — a memory companion that helps you stay close to the people who matter, especially when remembering becomes harder. It supports seniors in staying oriented and connected, and helps busy individuals stay human in a fast-moving world.

Revivo holds your story, nudges you when someone needs you, and keeps your relationships alive.

Remember. Reconnect. Revive.

## 🎨 Logo Direction

Think of the logo as something that feels calm, human, and gently restorative.

Logo Symbol Ideas

A soft loop or swirl forming a subtle “R” — representing the recall → reconnect → revive cycle.

Two overlapping circles — symbolizing relationships reconnecting.

A small spark within a circle — memory igniting connection.

A branching line (like a neural path) converging into a heart or dot — memory leading to a point of connection.

Style

Rounded corners

No harsh geometric shapes

Slightly organic forms

Friendly without being childish

Works well as a standalone icon on a phone dock

## 🌈 Color Palette

You want warmth + trust + clarity — not tech blue, not clinical white.

Primary Color

Warm Teal / Seafoam (#4BB8A9)
Calming, reassuring, universal, hints of life and renewal.

Secondary Colors

Soft Gold (#F3C776) – warmth and humanity

Gentle Coral (#F28F78) – emotion, relationships

Slate Grey (#475B66) – grounding, readability

Background / Neutrals

Porcelain (#F7F9FA)

Cloud Grey (#E3E7EA)

This gives your app a feeling of calm clarity rather than AI futurism.

## ✍️ UI Microcopy Tone Guide

Revivo should speak like a warm companion — clear, gentle, brief.

Tone Pillars

Warm (never robotic)
Encouraging (never commanding)
Human-first (never technical-first)
Calm (no urgency unless safety-related)
Examples

### Nudges:

“You haven’t spoken to Anita in a while — want to reach out?”
“You captured a beautiful moment yesterday. Add it to a memory?”
“It’s Rohan’s birthday next week. A simple message could make his day.”

Memory notifications:
“Here’s something you might want to remember.”
“You mentioned this person last month — here’s what you shared.”
“A moment from last year resurfaced.”

Reconnection moments:

“This might be a good time to reconnect.”
“A gentle reminder: your connections grow when you nurture them.”

For seniors / dementia context:

“Here’s a familiar face.”
“You’ve met Priya before — here’s how you know her.”
“Would you like help remembering this moment?”
